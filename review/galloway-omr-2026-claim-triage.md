# Galloway OMR 2026 Claim Triage

## Status

This page triages a user-provided conference-coverage text about Scott Galloway's OMR 2026 predictions. It is not a public source card. The text is useful as a claim intake and narrative map, but not as evidence for the public GitHub vault.

Rule for use:

- No protected article text.
- No claims copied as facts without public backing.
- Predictions stay marked as predictions.
- Market calls are not labour-market evidence.

## Short framing

Galloway's OMR predictions are best read as a high-signal provocation bundle:

- AI hype and infrastructure economics
- platform power
- media and attention shifts
- education as labour-market hedge
- synthetic relationships and social trust

For this vault, the useful move is not to adopt the predictions. The useful move is to separate what can be used as evidence, what is only a signal, and what should remain outside the employability frame.

## What is belastbar enough for the vault

### 1. AI is infrastructure-bound

The hard claim is not "the data-centre bubble will burst". The hard claim is weaker and more useful:

```text
AI adoption is constrained by compute, electricity, capital expenditure, grid capacity and local political acceptance.
```

This is relevant because infrastructure cost can shape which firms can use advanced AI, which workflows are affordable, and where automation concentrates.

Public anchors:

- IEA, Energy and AI: data-centre electricity demand grows strongly, but remains a share of the broader power system.
- EIA, Annual Energy Outlook / data-centre demand scenarios: regional electricity systems can react differently to rising data-centre load.
- Goldman Sachs Research: hyperscaler reinvestment and data-centre power demand are major investment and infrastructure questions.

Vault use:

- AI divide
- sustainable AI use
- platform concentration
- cost of automation

### 2. "AI creates jobs" is not a safe standalone claim

The vault should not write "AI will create more jobs than it destroys" as if it were established.

Better:

```text
AI changes task bundles; some jobs shrink, some grow, and new coordination, governance, review and infrastructure roles emerge.
```

This matches the existing vault frame:

- [Task Rebundling](../wiki/concepts/task-rebundling.md)
- [Entry Level Compression](../wiki/concepts/entry-level-compression.md)
- [Agentic Workflow Literacy](../wiki/concepts/agentic-workflow-literacy.md)

### 3. Education remains a credible hedge

The education point is more usable than the net-jobs point. The safe framing:

```text
Education does not protect because it certifies tool use. It protects when it builds abstraction, judgement, domain knowledge, social navigation and the ability to learn new systems.
```

Vault use:

- employability is not tool fluency
- portfolio evidence
- reserve skills
- human bottleneck skills

### 4. Media work is under platform and AI pressure

The Hollywood / short-form / AI claim is not a precise job forecast. It is useful as a media-work direction:

```text
Short-form platforms, creator economics and generative video reduce the monopoly of classic studios over attention and production capacity.
```

For design this supports:

- [Platform-led Creative Automation](../wiki/concepts/platform-led-creative-automation.md)
- [Designarbeit verschiebt sich zu Systemen](../wiki/packets/designarbeit-verschiebt-sich-zu-systemen.md)
- [Design Arbeitsmarkt Synthese](../wiki/syntheses/design-arbeitsmarkt-synthese.md)

## What should not become a vault claim

- specific valuation targets
- "bubble will burst" as prediction
- exact future market-cap comparisons
- investment recommendations
- geopolitical migration predictions
- broad social-collapse claims without public data
