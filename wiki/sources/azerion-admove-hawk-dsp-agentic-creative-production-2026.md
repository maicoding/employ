# Azerion AdMove.ai and Hawk DSP 2026: Agentic Creative Production in Media Buying

## Status

Evidence-linked for Azerion's public announcement of AdMove.ai integration into Hawk DSP. Use as vendor-side workflow signal, not as independent evidence of labour-market impact.

## Signal

Creative asset production is moving into media-buying infrastructure. Azerion plans to fully integrate AdMove.ai into Hawk DSP in Q2 2026, allowing agencies and advertisers to build, manage and launch creative assets in the same environment where media is bought.

## Evidence

- Azerion announced the full integration of AdMove.ai into Hawk DSP in Q2 2026.
- AdMove.ai is described as an AI agent platform for agencies managing complex multi-brand campaigns.
- Azerion says the platform supports the creative lifecycle from research and scripting to production and publishing.
- Azerion emphasizes brand standards, strategic frameworks, tone-of-voice guides, approval processes and separate brand/client environments.
- AdMove.ai positions itself as an autonomous AI agent for ad creative operations, including brand hub, creative strategy, asset generation, publishing and testing.

## Interpretation

The key employability signal is convergence: creative operations, programmatic media buying, brand governance and AI-agent supervision are becoming one workflow.

Skill implications:

- Creative Operations
- Programmatic and DSP Literacy
- Brand Governance
- AI Agent Supervision
- Performance Creative and Testing
- Rights, Safety and Compliance in scaled asset production

## Limit

This is vendor communication. It supports a workflow and market-direction signal, not independent claims about productivity, quality or job displacement.
