# Atlassian AI Design 2026: Non-determinism, Trust and Design Foundations

## Status

Evidence-linked for public Atlassian and Figma product/context sources. Use as workflow and practice signal, not as independent labour-market evidence.

## Signal

AI changes design because systems become non-deterministic. The employability signal is not simply "use AI tools", but design for uncertainty, trust, control, reversibility, mental models and workflow governance.

## Evidence

- Atlassian documents Rovo, Rovo Agents and Brand Voice Crafter as AI-assisted workflow infrastructure.
- Atlassian documents workflows combining Figma MCP, Atlassian Design System MCP and Rovo Dev CLI.
- Figma documents MCP as a way to provide design context to agentic tools.
- The combined pattern is clear enough for a cautious employability signal: designers increasingly work on systems where AI agents need context, constraints, review and recovery paths.

## Skill implications

- Non-deterministic UX
- Trust by design
- Agent state visibility
- Undo and recovery design
- Design-system literacy for agents and MCP
- Multimodal interaction design beyond chat
- Brand voice and marketing workflow governance
- Design history and visual foundations as judgement criteria

## Limit

Atlassian is a vendor. Use the interview as expert/practice signal, not as neutral evidence of productivity or labour-market impact.
