# AGF and Amazon Video Measurement: Platform Metrics as Employability Signal

## Status

Evidence-linked for the Amazon / Prime Video benchmark, based on public AGF and Nielsen documentation.

## Signal

Video work is increasingly shaped by measurement infrastructure. The key labour-market signal is not "more video" alone, but the ability to understand who defines reach, comparability and market currency.

## Evidence

- AGF and Nielsen documented Prime Video's integration into AGF measurement in late 2025.
- The Amazon integration uses a hybrid method combining panel data with census data from a server-to-server integration.
- AGF describes this as the first time a Joint Industry Committee integrated an international streaming service into a standardized measurement system via server-to-server technology.

## Interpretation

Measurement is market power. Platform dashboards help optimize inside a platform, but advertisers, broadcasters and agencies need cross-silo comparability to allocate budgets and judge reach.

Employability implications:

- read platform metrics critically
- distinguish reach, watch time, frequency, interactions and net reach
- understand AGF, Nielsen, adserver, DSP and platform-dashboard boundaries
- report campaign results with method notes
- treat measurement assumptions as part of portfolio evidence

## Limit

Do not use this source to claim that all large video platforms are neutrally measured. Use it as a signal that neutral, comparable measurement is becoming a contested infrastructure layer.
